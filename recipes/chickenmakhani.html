Chicken Makhani(butter chicken)

Description

Chicken Makhani, widely known as Butter Chicken, is one of the most beloved and iconic dishes of Indian cuisine, originating in Delhi in the 1950s. This dish features tender pieces of chicken that have been marinated, usually in yogurt and spices, and then cooked in a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato sauce.

The hallmark of Chicken Makhani is its luxurious gravy. It achieves its characteristic velvety texture and slightly sweet, tangy flavor through the use of pureed tomatoes, a generous amount of butter (hence the name), and cream. A balanced blend of spices, including garam masala, fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi), and sometimes a touch of sugar, ensures a complex but non-spicy profile. It is the quintessential comfort food, typically served with naan bread or basmati rice to soak up the decadent sauce.

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
    •

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, cumin, coriander, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger paste and garlic paste (about 1 tablespoon combined) and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook until the chicken is no longer pink.
  5. Add the tomato puree and cook for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  6. Stir in the remaining butter, heavy cream, garam masala, and dried fenugreek leaves. Simmer for another 10 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with naan or basmati rice.
