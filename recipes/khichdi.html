Moong Dal Khichdi

Description

Khichdi is a healthy, nourishing, and easily digestible dish made from a mixture of rice and lentils (dal). It is a staple comfort food across India, often prepared as a light meal or when one needs something simple. This recipe uses yellow split moong dal and is typically served with a generous spoonful of ghee.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup Basmati Rice or Short-grain Rice
  • 1/4 cup Yellow Split Moong Dal
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt
  • 4 cups Water
  • 2 tablespoons Ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida
  • 2-3 Green Chilies, slit
  • 1 inch piece Ginger, grated
  • A few Curry Leaves
  • Extra Ghee, for topping
  • Plain Yogurt or Pickle

Steps

  1. Prepare Rice and Dal: Rinse the rice and moong dal together 2-3 times until the water runs clear. Soak them together in fresh water for about 30 minutes (optional, but helps speed up cooking).
  2. Pressure Cook: Drain the rice and dal and transfer them to a pressure cooker. Add the turmeric powder, salt, and 4 cups of water. Close the lid and cook for 3-4 whistles (or until very soft and mushy).
  3. Prepare Tempering (Tadka): In a small pan, heat the ghee (or oil) over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and allow them to splutter.
  4. Add Aromatics: Add the asafoetida (Hing), green chilies, and grated ginger. Saut√© for about 30 seconds until the ginger is fragrant. Add the curry leaves (if using).
  5. Combine: Pour the hot tempering mixture directly into the cooked khichdi in the pressure cooker. Mix well. If the khichdi is too thick, add a little hot water to reach your desired consistency and simmer for 2 minutes.
  6. Serve: Serve the Khichdi immediately in a bowl, topped with a generous spoonful of hot ghee and accompanied by yogurt or pickle.
