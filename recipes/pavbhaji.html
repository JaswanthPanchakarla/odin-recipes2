Pav Bhaji

Description

Pav Bhaji is a beloved Indian street food originating from Mumbai, featuring a rich, spicy, and tangy mashed vegetable curry known as 'Bhaji,' served alongside soft, butter-toasted bread rolls called 'Pav.' It is a hearty, flavorful dish perfect for lunch, dinner, or a quick snack.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium Potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup Cauliflower florets, chopped
  • 1 cup Green Peas
  • 1 large Carrot, chopped
  • 4 tablespoons Butter (plus extra for toasting pav)
  • 1 tablespoon Oil
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 1 medium Onion, finely chopped
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum), finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 cups Tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji Masala
  • 1-2 teaspoons Kashmiri Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water
  • 8 Pav
  • Lemon wedges, chopped onion, and fresh cilantro (for garnish)
    •

Steps

  1. Cook the Vegetables: In a pressure cooker or large pot, combine the potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrot with water. Cook until the vegetables are very soft. Mash them coarsely with a potato masher and set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pan or tawa, heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil. Add the cumin seeds. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Build the Masala: Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 30 seconds. Next, add the bell pepper and cook until soft, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add the chopped tomatoes and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are completely mushy. Add the Pav Bhaji Masala, chili powder, and turmeric. Mix well and cook for 1 minute.
  5. Combine and Mash: Add the mashed vegetables to the pan. Use the potato masher again to mix everything together and mash until the bhaji reaches a semi-smooth consistency. Add water to adjust the thickness.
  6. Simmer and Finish: Simmer the bhaji for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. Stir in the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter. Taste and adjust salt or masala.
  7. Toast the Pav: Slit the pav horizontally. Heat a small amount of butter on a flat pan. Toast the pav on both sides until golden brown.
  8. Serve: Serve the hot bhaji topped with a dollop of butter, chopped cilantro, and raw onions, accompanied by the toasted pav and a lemon wedge.
